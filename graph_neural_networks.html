Graph Neural Networks

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are a type of Neural Network that operate directly on graphs. They are capable of processing the nodes and edges of a graph, as well as any associated attributes. This makes GNNs particularly suited to tasks where the data can naturally be represented as a graph, such as social network analysis, recommendation systems, and molecule design.

Equation: \( H^{(l+1)} = \sigma( D^{-1} A H^{(l)} W^{(l)} ) \)

The equation above describes the propagation rule of a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN), a popular type of GNN. Here, \( H^{(l)} \) represents the node features at layer \( l \), \( A \) is the adjacency matrix of the graph, \( D \) is the degree matrix of the graph, \( W^{(l)} \) are the trainable weights at layer \( l \), and \( \sigma \) is the activation function.
